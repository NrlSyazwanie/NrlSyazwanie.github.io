Nasi Lemak

Nasi Lemak is a Malay food dish. It is made from rice cooked with coconut milk, onion, ginger and pandan leaves. Nasi Lemak is also known as Malaysia's national dish and can be found in every corner of Malaysia.Nasi lemak in Malaysia usually contains "sambal tumis", fried anchovies, fried peanuts, cucumber and boiled eggs. There are also Nasi Lemak sellers who serve it with other side dishes such as "Sambal sotong", "sambal kerang", fried egg, fried chicken, rendang and so on.

Asam Laksa

Asam Laksa is a traditional dish of the Baba and Nyonya community. This dish is rice noodles served with spicy fish gravy. The descendants of Baba and Nyonya were originally Chinese immigrants who came to Malaya. They have incorporated Malay culture applied in daily life.The combination of elements of these two cultures has resulted in this Asam Laksa dish. This dish is eaten with other ingredients such as sliced ​​cucumber, onion, chilli, pineapple, salad leaves, kesum leaves and kantan flowers.

Roti Canai

Roti Canai is a flat bread introduced by the Indian community. It can be found in most mamak restaurants and Malay restaurants in Malaysia. This dish is brought by Tamil Muslim immigrants who have settled in Malaysia. It is also known as ‘flying bread’ because of its making process which is like throwing and round before cooking. Usually Roti Canai is eaten with curry gravy whether it is fish, chicken, meat or goat curry, dhal bean gravy and also sauteed sambal. In fact, some places enjoy this Roti Canai with granulated sugar mixed into curry sauce. This Roti Canai also has various other options such as Roti Telur, Roti Tisu, Roti Pisang, Roti Bawang, Roti Planta and so on.

Rojak

Rojak is a traditional salad that contains a variety of fruits and vegetables. In Malaysia, there are various types of rojak, namely fruit rojak, Indian rojak, Penang rojak and kangkung squid rojak. For fruit rojak usually consists of pieces of cucumber, mango, pineapple, sengkuang, bean sprouts and also fried tofu. Rojak buah gravy is made from soy sauce, belacan, water, sugar, lime juice, chilli and ground peanuts.While the Indian rojak is known as pasembor or rojak mamak. This rojak gravy is a thick peanut gravy that tastes sweet and spicy. This rojak consists of boiled potatoes, boiled eggs, bean sprouts, sengkuang and cucumber. It also contains fried tofu, fried cucur, fried shrimp or squid.

Apam Balik

Apam Balik is a type of flat pancake that is cooked using a special mold and can be found anywhere around Malaysia. It is also widely sold by hawkers everywhere. The ingredients used to make apam balik consist of a mixture of flour, coconut milk, water, sugar, eggs and baking soda. It has fillings like ground peanuts, sugar and sweet corn cream. Apam balik can also be placed with various other types of filling such as chocolate slices, grated coconut and so on according to their tastes.

Mee Kari

Mee Kari is a flavorful and unique dish from a combination of Chinese and Indian dishes. The broth is made from curry spices mixed with coconut milk, chili paste, dried tofu, shrimp, chicken, squid, mussels, lemongrass and mint leaves. This dish has become a favorite of many regardless of race and is easily available at local restaurants. The delicious curry sauce can be eaten with noodles, kuetiaw or vermicelli and can be eaten with sour chillies or pickled chillies.

Sate

Satay is a slice of meat that is marinated, pricked with a skewer and roasted on a charcoal stove. Usually satay is served with peanut gravy and also sliced ​​onion and cucumber. There are various types of satay in Malaysia, namely meat satay, chicken satay, stomach satay and goat satay. It is a favorite of almost all Malaysians and can be easily found at hawker stalls, night markets and restaurants.

Char Kuey Teow

Char Kuew Teow is a very popular food that can be found in Malaysia. Usually it is fried on high heat with liquid soy sauce, thick soy sauce, chilli, shrimp paste, shrimp, mussels, bean sprouts and chives. In addition, Char Kuew Teow is also fried along with eggs, shrimp, mussels, bean sprouts and more. This dish is also served on banana leaves placed in a plate to further enhance the aroma.

Nasi Kandar

Nasi kandar is a very popular dish and comes from Penang. It is a white rice dish served with curry sauce and additional side dishes. Usually it is eaten with fried chicken, goat curry, fried squid, fried shrimp, fried fish, beef and so on. It is also often complemented with vegetables such as fried bitter gourd, fried cabbage, side dishes and so on. Almost all communities are very fond of Nasi Kandar no matter where.
